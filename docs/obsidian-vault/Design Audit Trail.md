# Design Audit Trail

**Read this out loud in 4 points:**

1. **After all features were functional, I ran a systematic design audit — not random polish, but finding-by-finding issue tracking.** Each finding got an ID (FINDING-001), a description, a specific fix, and its own atomic commit. This means any individual fix can be reverted without touching others.

2. **Seven specific findings were identified and fixed.** Inconsistent H3 sizes, CTA heading mismatch, below-fold text contrast too low (neutral-400 → neutral-600), touch targets under 44px, left-border "AI slop" on cards, tiny + bullets in pricing, nav button too short.

3. **A separate pass broke template-level AI patterns.** Beyond individual pixel issues, the overall layout had tells: perfectly symmetric 3-card grids, identical section rhythm, uniform card styling. These were replaced with asymmetric layouts, alternating section spacing, and a mix of bordered/borderless/elevated cards.

4. **The final push targeted A grades across 5 dimensions.** Touch targets (all ≥44px), hover states (every clickable element), motion accessibility (reduced-motion respected), layout asymmetry (no template-feeling grids), checkmarks (consistent style). Each dimension was independently graded.

---

## If they probe deeper

**"What's 'AI slop'?"** — Left-border colored accent on cards. Every AI code generator defaults to this pattern. It looks fine individually but screams "generated by AI" when repeated across a page. Removing it and using clean borders is a subtle but important differentiation.

**"Why audit at 4 AM?"** — Design polish happens after features work. The audit happened 10+ hours into the build, after scoring, matching, rubric, and export were all functional. Polish on a working product is more efficient than polishing wireframes.

**"How do you grade touch targets?"** — Measure the rendered click/tap area of every interactive element. WCAG 2.5.5 requires ≥44×44px. Found 3 elements under this threshold: two pricing buttons and the nav "Get Started" button. Added padding to bring them up.
